Unifying Principle

Global Constraint Rule

The scientific foundation unifying MediBalans patent portfolio and clinical methodology.

Global Constraint Rule (GCR) is a systems biology principle describing how biological systems — from individual cells to entire organs — always operate under prevailing constraints. När en begränsning är tillräckligt allvarlig, omkonfigureras hela systemet för att kompensera.

The clinical problem is that the visible dysfunction is rarely the primary constraint. Conventional medicine often treats the secondary compensation — and wonders why the effect is absent or recurs.

The GCR framework guides MediBalans diagnostics: we systematically search for the biological root constraint driving a condition, regardless of where it manifests clinically. This is the principle behind why the same symptom can require entirely different treatment in different patients.

Seven patents have so far emerged from this framework — in longevity medicine, regenerative therapies and precision nutrition.

Global Constraint Rule · Core Definition

Biological systems always adapt to their most limiting factor — not their most obvious one.

Identifying and correcting the root constraint is more effective than treating the adaptive compensations it has generated.

The same symptom can have fundamentally different biological origins
Treating compensations gives temporary effect — treating the constraint gives lasting results
Multi-omics diagnostics can identify the constraint with high precision
Protocol design must be adapted to the individual's specific constraint profile
