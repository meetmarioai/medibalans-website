Why IV & IM?

Why IV therapy
is more effective

Vitamins and minerals taken orally are absorbed via the gut — a process limited by intestinal integrity, genotype, competition between substances and saturation mechanisms. Vitamin C absorbs at only 10–30% at high oral doses; intestinal absorption saturates at around 200 mg and the rest is excreted.

Oral glutathione is even more problematic. Peptidases in the gastrointestinal tract degrade the tripeptide before it can be absorbed intact — under 5% reaches systemic circulation as intact glutathione. IV infusion and IM injection bypass this barrier entirely.

At MediBalans, all IV and IM treatments are administered by a licensed physician after individual assessment. The physician selects the right substance, dose and route of administration based on your clinical picture.

Glutathione IV
Stockholm —
what, why, how

Gamma-L-glutamyl-L-cysteinylglycine · GSH · Tripeptide

Glutathione is the body's primary intracellular antioxidant — a tripeptide of glutamate, cysteine and glycine. Every cell produces and consumes glutathione continuously as part of its oxidative defence, liver detoxification and immune function.

In chronic inflammation, high toxin exposure, oxidative stress, intense training or systemic illness, glutathione is consumed faster than it can be synthesised. Depleted glutathione is a recurring finding in ME/CFS, Long COVID, liver disease and autoimmunity.

Oral glutathione does not work. Peptidases in the gut break down the tripeptide; under 5% is absorbed intact. IV infusion and IM injection deliver glutathione directly into systemic circulation and liver cells.

What glutathione does in the body

Antioxidant
Neutralises reactive oxygen species (ROS) and prevents oxidative damage to DNA, proteins and cell membranes.
Liver detoxification
Conjugates toxins and heavy metals in phase II detoxification (GST enzymes). Critical for hepatic detox capacity.
Immune modulation
Regulates T-cell function, NK-cell activity and cytokine production. Depleted GSH is associated with immune suppression.
Energy metabolism
Protects mitochondrial integrity from oxidative damage. Supports ATP production via oxidative phosphorylation.
Antioxidant network
Recycles oxidised vitamin C (dehydroascorbate) and vitamin E back to active forms. Amplifies the entire antioxidant system.
