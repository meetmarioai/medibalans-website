What Is NLS?

Bioresonance at cellular level

NLS — Non-Linear System Analysis — is a technology that measures electromagnetic bioresonance in the body's organs and tissues without needles, radiation or invasive methods.

Every cell in the body emits weak electromagnetic signals. NLS technology captures these signals via headphones with built-in sensors and compares them against a reference database of frequencies associated with various biological states.

The result is a broad mapping of potential imbalances in organs, glands, joints, the lymphatic system and nervous system — not a diagnosis, but a biological landscape that gives us clinical hypotheses to test further.

The critical step is what happens after the scan. NLS findings are always confirmed with targeted laboratory testing — blood, urine, stool or genetic analysis — before treatment begins. We treat what we can measure and verify.

Clinical Position

NLS is an orientation map, not a conclusion. Its value lies in efficiently directing deeper investigation towards the systems signalling imbalance — saving time for the patient.
